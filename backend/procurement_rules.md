# Procurement Rules and Regulations

## 1. General Financial Rules (GFR) 2017 - Rule 170
- **Bid Security (EMD)**: Mandatory for all procurements above ₹5,00,000.
- **Amount**: Typically 2% to 5% of the estimated value of the goods/services.
- **Exemptions**: MSMEs and Startups are exempted from EMD if they provide a Bid Security Declaration.

## 2. GeM (Government e-Marketplace) GTC
- **Bid Validity**: Standard bid validity should be minimum 90 days.
- **Delivery Period**: Should be realistic; typically 30-45 days for standard goods.
- **Payment Terms**: 100% payment within 10-30 days of generation of Consignee Receipt and Acceptance Certificate (CRAC).

## 3. CVC (Central Vigilance Commission) Guidelines
- **Restrictive Clauses**: Avoid clauses that restrict competition (e.g., extremely high turnover requirements).
- **Turnover**: Annual turnover requirement should not ideally exceed 2x of the estimated tender value.
- **Experience**: Minimum experience requirement typically ranges from 1 to 3 years depending on complexity.

## 4. Conflict of Interest
- Bidders having common directors or ownership with other bidders in the same tender are disqualified.
- Direct or indirect relationship with the procurement officer must be declared.

## 5. Ambiguity and Clarity
- Avoid vague terms like "approx", "flexible", "TBD", or "as per requirement".
- Technical specifications must be precise and measurable.

## 6. Language Requirements
- All official communications and tender documents for the Government of Andhra Pradesh must be available in English.
- Local language (Telugu) summaries are encouraged for broader accessibility.
